Mai Jiaying Xu

I am a second-year Master's student in Psychology at Brandeis University, mentored by Dr. Raymond Knight. After transferring from the University of Washington, I earned my Bachelor's degree in Psychology and Philosophy from Brandeis in 2023, under the guidance of Dr. James Lackner.

My research interests began in high school, where I explored how homeless women’s behaviors differ across socioeconomic statuses. I further developed my research skills in 2021 as a research assistant at the Chinese National Academy of Sciences, examining the relationship between poverty, women's parenting behaviors, and happiness. At the Knight Lab, I have focused on sexual aggression, psychopathology, and substance abuse, with my Master’s thesis exploring alcohol as a predictor of sexual aggression recidivism.

I am deeply passionate about clinical and developmental psychology, particularly in the early detection and intervention of psychological disorders. My goal is to identify early risk factors and develop preventative strategies to mitigate severe psychological outcomes.

Outside of research, I enjoy creative activities such as baking, painting, playing the Guzheng, and ice skating. I am committed to fostering warmth and positivity in both my personal and professional life.
