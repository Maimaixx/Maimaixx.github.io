Mai Jiaying Xu

I am a Research Fellow and Lab Manager at the State Key Laboratory of Cognitive Science & Mental Health (Chinese Academy of Sciences). I lead multi-method studies on childhood adversity, resilience, and intervention mechanisms across large-scale datasets, clinical adolescent cohorts, and juvenile intervention trials.

My current work examines the psychological and neurobiological impacts of early adversity, including links with inflammatory biomarkers and mental health outcomes. I focus on protective traits that preserve executive function and use neuroimaging to track developmental trajectories.

I collaborate with Harvard's Lab for Youth Mental Health (intervention meta-analyses) and the Emotion and Psychopathology in Context Lab (cross-cultural emotion regulation meta).
